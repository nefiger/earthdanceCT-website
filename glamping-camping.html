Glamping & camping

Earthdance is a multi-day camping gathering, and spending the weekend on site is part of the experience. Bring your own setup, walk into a ready-made tent, or enquire directly about the one-only Morado Experience.

Staying at Earthdance

Wake up inside the gathering

Camping keeps people close to the rhythm of the weekend: the music, the mornings, the slower moments in between, and the shared atmosphere of being together in one place. You don't drive home from Earthdance at 2am — you walk back to your tent under the stars, and the farm is still there when you unzip in the morning.

For 2026, five camp and glamping options are bookable now on Quicket, on the same event page as festival tickets: bring-your-own camping with your car beside your tent from R350, and ready-set canvas glamping tents for two from R3,000. The Morado Experience is a separate, direct-enquiry stay for two. Every option is described below.

BYO Camp & Park

Bring your own tent and camping setup, with space to camp next to your car

For guests who prefer their own camping setup but want the convenience of keeping their vehicle close by.

A practical, flexible choice for those arriving with their own tent, mattress, bedding, chairs and camping equipment. This option does not include a supplied tent, mattress, bedding or shade structure — you are responsible for your full camping setup.

A ready-set canvas tent for two, with bedding included

A simple, comfortable festival stay without needing to bring your own tent or bedding.

Your ready-set waterproof canvas tent is up before you arrive, with mattresses, a fitted sheet, comforter and two pillows with covers included. This is the most compact glamping option — a pre-built sleeping space that keeps things practical and affordable.

  • Includes waterproof canvas tent for two, mattresses, fitted sheet, comforter, two pillows with covers, rechargeable tent light, charging access, shared social space, staff support

The Morado Experience

Our most exclusive Earthdance stay — a private sanctuary beside the dam, moments from the heart of the festival.

A little extra space to breathe

A slower rhythm within the weekend

Morado brings together Kromrivier Farm's beautiful private cottage and a collection of thoughtful touches created especially for the Earthdance community.

Nestled beside its own private dam, with sweeping mountain and farmland views, it is a place to begin your mornings slowly, retreat between adventures, and unwind beneath a sky full of stars before returning to the dancefloor.

Private accommodation for two

Room to reconnect

Designed for two guests, Morado features a queen-sized bed with premium linen, a fully equipped self-catering kitchen, spacious indoor living area, fireplace and complimentary Wi-Fi.

Large sliding doors open onto a private garden with a braai area and wood-fired hot tub overlooking the dam. A freestanding bath and open rainfall shower take in the surrounding landscape, while direct access to the Hartbeesberg hiking trail invites you to explore the farm at your own pace.

How Booking Works

Clear options, no guesswork

The five camp and glamping options are booked through Quicket, on the same event page as festival tickets. The Morado Experience is available by direct enquiry only.

  • Each Quicket option is listed separately with its own price and inclusions — check the listing for full details.
  • Glamping tents sleep two guests each, and capacity is limited: if you want one, book early.
  • For Morado, contact the Earthdance team directly to ask about availability and booking.
  • Accommodation guests will receive arrival and check-in guidance closer to the event, and the same information will be published here.
Before You Book

Know what you're walking into

Glamping covers your bed, not your kitchen: the shared social space has tables and charging points, but you bring your own plates, cutlery, cups and cookware.

The BYO Camp & Park option gives you space to camp beside your car — your full camping setup is yours to bring.

Whatever you choose, read Practical Info for what to pack: spring on the farm means warm days and properly cold nights.
